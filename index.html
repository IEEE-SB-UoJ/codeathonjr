Empowering Young Minds with IoT

Innovate, Create, and Lead with Codeathon Jr!

Our Mission

Codeathon Jr. organized by the IEEE Student Branch at the University of Jaffna, is designed to introduce grade 10 students in Jaffna to the transformative potential of the Internet of Things (IoT). The initiative includes interactive IoT sessions, distribution of IoT kits, practical training, and a final Makeathon competition to foster innovation and problem-solving skills.

Objectives

1

Empower A/L Students : Empower A/L students in five schools in Jaffna with practical IoT knowledge and skills.

2

Encourage STEM Engagement : Foster interest and engagement in STEM subjects among A/L students in the targeted schools.

3

Community Impact : Extend the benefits of IoT education to the broader community by showcasing innovative projects during the Makeathon.

4

Promote Collaboration : Encourage collaborative problem-solving and teamwork among students during the Makeathon.

    The Makeathon competition takes place. Student projects are evaluated by the judging panel based on innovation, functionality, and problem-solving effectiveness.

How We Select Schools for IoT Training Sessions

To ensure the most impactful and engaging experience, we select schools based on the following criteria:

STEM Engagement

  1. Schools with a demonstrated commitment to STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) education.
  2. Priority is given to schools that have integrated STEM subjects effectively into their curriculum.

Student Performance

  • Schools with higher pass rates and active participation in STEM-related activities.
  • Emphasis on schools that encourage and support student participation in science fairs, competitions, and extracurricular STEM programs.

    Each school will receive three MagicBit IoT kits each worth of Rs. 18,000. These kits are equipped with everything needed to continue exploring IoT projects and experiments beyond the training sessions.

    Expert-led workshops that provide a comprehensive introduction to IoT concepts and applications tailored for school students.

    Interactive Workshops

    Interactive sessions that teach the fundamentals of IoT using Microbit microcontrollers, incorporating collaborative group work.

    Makeathon Competition

    Opportunity for students to participate in a Makeathon competition, applying their IoT knowledge to develop innovative solutions for real-world problems.

    Answers to the most frequently asked questions.

    The MagicBit microcontroller is a beginner-friendly device designed for learning and experimenting with IoT. It supports visual programming, making it accessible for students to understand and create IoT projects.

    The training sessions will be conducted in Tamil to ensure full engagement and understanding by the students.

    The Makeathon is a competition where students apply their IoT knowledge to develop innovative solutions to real-world problems. It fosters teamwork, creativity, and critical thinking, and allows students to showcase their skills and ingenuity.

    The training session is a full-day event, typically running from 8:30 AM to 4:30 PM, with a lunch break in between.

    Students will learn the basics of IoT, including its concepts and applications, through interactive workshops and hands-on exercises with the MagicBit microcontroller. They will also participate in a practical Makeathon competition to apply their knowledge.

    School students upto A/L from selected schools in the Jaffna region are eligible to participate. Schools are chosen based on their engagement with STEM education, student performance in STEM subjects, and resource availability.

    At the conclusion of each training session, five students per school will be chosen for the Makeathon based on their performance in quizzes and group assignments.
